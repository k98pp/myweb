CHICKEN BIRYANI





Ingrediants:

    1:2cups Basmati rice
    500g chicken or vegetables
    2 onions, finely sliced
    2 tomatoes, chopped
    1/2 cup plain yogurt
    2 tablespoons biryani masala
    1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    4-5 cloves garlic, minced
    2-inch piece of ginger, minced
    4-5 green chilies, chopped
    1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
    1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
    Salt to taste
    2 tablespoons cooking oil
    Saffron strands soaked in 2 tablespoons of warm milk for garnis

Instruction:

    Wash and soak the Basmati rice in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
    In a large, heavy-bottomed pan, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    Add the sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden
    Add the minced garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Sauté for a few minutes until the raw smell disappears.
    Add the chicken or vegetables and cook until they are partially done.
    Stir in the biryani masala, chopped tomatoes, yogurt, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil begins to separate.
    Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken or vegetable mixture. Sprinkle half of the chopped coriander and mint leaves on top.
    Drizzle the saffron milk over the rice for a beautiful golden color.
    Cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid and simmer on low heat for about 20-25 minutes, until the rice is fully cooked and the flavors meld together.
    Garnish with the remaining coriander and mint leaves.
    Serve hot with raita or salad.
